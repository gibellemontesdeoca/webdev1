Ted Bundy- American Serial Killer

One of the most Famous Murderers of the 20th Century

Biography- Who was Ted Bundy?

Ted Bundy was born in Burlington, Vermont on November 244th. His mother Eleanor Loiuse gave birth to him in a home for unwed mothers and would try to hide the fact that he was an illegitimate child. She raised him as the adopted son of his grandparents and pretended to be his sister He had a difficult childhood and had a strange relationship with his stepfather. At a young age, Ted was a shy kid and that caused him to get bullied. However, he eventually developed more social skills and embraced his intellegence which allowed him to have a successful college career. He also began dating and became involved in normal emotional relationships throughout the years. He graduated college by 1952 and showed great promise in a career in law or politics but his true passion came to light shortly after

Crime

Ted Bundy sexually assaulted and killed many young women in Washington, Oregon, Colorado, Utah, and Florida between the years 1974-1978. Although he confessed to 28 murders, he is suspected of hundreds. He intended to kill young, attractive women and he put on this persona as a disabled man by walking in crutches or having a fake cast. He would gain the trust of these women by often times helping them unload things into their car. He would sometimes take on the persona of a cop or someone of authority to further gain trust. Once he could get them in his car, he would strike them over the head with a crowbar or pipe which would knock them out for a while. During this time, he would rape the women as the were unconscious. He typically strangled and mutilated his victims. He escaped police custody once and immediately went on to kill at least 5 more women in Florida.

The Trial

After escaping police custody twice, he was arrested again on February 15 for stolen license plates. This was shortly after he killed and raped Kimberly Leach who went missing from school, and who many believe was his last victim. The testimony of one of his survivors had dental evidence that directly linked him to the attack. The jury returned a verdict of guilty, and he sentenced Ted Bundy to death twice in the form of the electric chair. At this point, there was an overwhelming amount of forensic evidence and eye witnesses. The jury once again announced a guilty verdict and he was put to death by electrocution on January 24, in Florida where he died at the age of 42.
